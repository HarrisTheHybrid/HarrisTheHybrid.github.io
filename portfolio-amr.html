Robot Description

A mobile autonomous robot was designed as part of the Autonomous Mobile Robotics course and concept competition during the 2020 Fall at Cooper Union. The robot’s basic objectives are to maneuver around a defined space while being able to avoidobstacles, including rocks and Bonzai of various sizes. The scoring objectives for the robot are to shoot missiles, ping pong balls in this case, at the opponent robot, and beable to deliver the missiles to a receiving hole at the opponent’s base, in which case thelocation of the hole is unknown. To achieve the objectives mentioned above, a three layered structure chassis is designed, in order to separate the driving system, the control unit, and the launching system. A swerve drive system is attached to the robot with four rotatable wheels that have enough grasp while being able to drive the robot in any direction. Four servo motors and four dc motors are used as part of the design for the swerve drive system. A ping pong ball launcher with two parts are designed. The first part is a launching exit that can move up and down to match the height of the launching hole at the opponent’s base. The second part is a rack and pinion system driven by a servo motor that can add momentum to the ping pong ball being launched. A tof camera is utilized in the design as the major sensing components for the robotto detect obstacles and opponents. To mitigate the vision field limit imposed by tof camera, ultrasonic sensors are supplemented. Overall the robot design demonstrates a mobile autonomous robot that could satisfy the given constraints using the cuttingedge technologies.
